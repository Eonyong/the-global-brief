About The Global Brief

Independent, bilingual analysis of the stories shaping our world — published twice daily in English and Korean.

Our Mission

The Global Brief was created to bridge the gap between surface-level news aggregation and the kind of deep, contextual analysis that readers need to truly understand global affairs.

We publish two analytical articles every day — one in the morning covering politics, and one in the afternoon covering economics. Every article is available in both English and Korean, reflecting our commitment to serving both global and Korean-language audiences.

What We Cover

  • Global Politics — US policy, EU affairs, Asia-Pacific tensions, Middle East diplomacy, elections, and international institutions
  • World Economy — Central bank policy, financial markets, technology economy, energy, trade, and emerging markets
  • Korea Focus — Korean Peninsula geopolitics, Korean economy, KOSPI, Samsung, and Korea's role in global affairs

Our Approach

We believe that good journalism is analytical, not just descriptive. Each article asks not just "what happened?" but "why does it matter?" and "what comes next?"

Our articles are structured for busy, educated readers who want real depth without jargon. We aim for the analytical clarity of The Economist combined with the strategic perspective of Foreign Affairs.

Bilingual Content

Every article on The Global Brief is published in both English and Korean. Simply click the 🇰🇷 한국어 button at the top of any article to switch languages.

Korean translations are crafted for natural readability — not machine-translated — ensuring that Korean readers receive the same quality analysis as English-speaking audiences.

Contact

For inquiries, feedback, or partnership opportunities, reach out to our editorial team. We value reader input and are committed to continuous improvement.
